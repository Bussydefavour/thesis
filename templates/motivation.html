Motivation

Research on ad click-through rate (CTR) prediction has focused heavily on individual machine learning and deep learning models. While these approaches have their merits, studies suggest an ensemble model could enhance prediction accuracy. For instance, Muthaiah Ramanathan's study demonstrated that an ensemble model (ROC AUC = 0.91) outperformed individual models such as logistic regression (ROC AUC = 0.9), XGBoost (ROC AUC = 0.84), Random Forest (ROC AUC = 0.84), and Decision Tree (ROC AUC = 0.79). However, his research was constrained by the limited size of the dataset, using only 300,000 observations out of a possible 700,000 due to computational demands.

As the need for accurate ad click predictions becomes more critical, expanding the dataset to enhance the ensemble method's accuracy is crucial. This research aims to build an ensemble model using a more extensive dataset, potentially improving the success of online marketing campaigns by minimizing inefficiencies and capitalizing on opportunities.
